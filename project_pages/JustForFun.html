What I do for fun

Interactive Arduino

I wanted to build a device I could just hand to people with no instructions and watch them learn how to interact with it. I built and programed an interactive device with Arduino.

This is a mood light. Users could turn the device on with the button. The first knob allowed users to change the colors that mix (blue and orange shown in the image) and the other knob allowed users to change the rate at which the colors fluctuate.

Pottery

I've been doing pottery for the past 3 years or so. I mainly throw on the wheel. I really like the combination of building with your hands and glazing. Its always an exciting time because I never know exactly how a piece is going to turn out.

Carvey

I wanted to learn to use the Carvey, a 3D tabletop carving machine. I first went to the short safety class to be able to use the machine. Then I started carving. The first three carves didn't go well but after making some changes and learning from my mistakes, I was finally able to get my flame carved. Once I figured things out, I carved on a new piece of wood and singed the edges to make the edges clean and give more of an impression of fire.

Tiny House Furniture

I like designing for small spaces because the tight constraint demands innovation. I worked on designing a tiny house that would facilitate social interaction, because that seemed like a major drawback of current designs.
